A Stable Right-Sided Quaternion Fourier Regime for Multichannel Spectral Analysis

This draft defines a one-dimensional fixed-axis right-sided quaternion Fourier regime for lanewise multichannel spectral processing and proves inversion and energy invariants.

John G. Van Geem
RQM Technologies

Abstract

This paper defines a stable right-sided discrete quaternion Fourier regime for multichannel spectral analysis. Building on canonical quaternion lanes, it adopts a fixed transform axis i and uses X[k] = Σn=0N-1 x[n] exp(-i2πnk/N), x[n] = (1/N)Σk=0N-1 X[k] exp(+i2πnk/N). The manuscript proves exact inversion, right-linearity over the commuting slice Ci, spectral power nonnegativity, and Parseval invariance. Scope is intentionally limited to this fixed public convention.

1. Introduction

Quaternion Fourier analysis admits multiple conventions (left-, right-, and two-sided variants). This paper declares one stable public seam: fixed axis i, right-sided kernel placement, one-dimensional index, and lanewise application to quaternion-lifted multichannel data.

2. Stable transform definition

Forward transform. X[k] = Σn=0N-1 x[n] exp(-i2πnk/N), for k=0,...,N-1.
Inverse transform. x[n] = (1/N)Σk=0N-1 X[k] exp(+i2πnk/N), for n=0,...,N-1.

3. Core algebraic results

  • Additivity and right Ci-linearity over commuting slice scalars.
  • Exact inversion using root-of-unity orthogonality in Ci.
  • Spectral power P[k] = |X[k]|2 is real and nonnegative.
  • Parseval identity: Σn|x[n]|2 = (1/N)Σk|X[k]|2.
  • Lanewise multichannel Parseval extension by summing lane identities.

4. Scope and non-claims

The paper does not claim universal superiority over other quaternion Fourier conventions and does not cover two-sided, higher-dimensional, or axis-adaptive transforms. It also defers cross-lane coherence/orientation metrics to the next paper.
