Quaternion Lifting for Multichannel RF and Sensing Signals

A canonical lane representation for quaternionic signal processing.

John G. Van Geem
RQM Technologies
ORCID: 0009-0002-4003-8452

Abstract

This paper defines a canonical quaternion lifting procedure for multichannel RF and sensing signals whose external intake remains standard complex-channel data. Given two complex channels z1[n]=a[n]+ib[n] and z2[n]=c[n]+id[n], the lift forms a quaternion lane q[n]=z1[n]+z2[n]j = a[n]+b[n]i+c[n]j+d[n]k. For larger captures, channels are grouped deterministically into quaternion lanes Q[g,n] in H. The paper formalizes this lift, its inverse unpacking map, odd-channel handling, and representation-level invariants that make it suitable as a canonical internal form. The contribution is representational rather than speculative: the lift preserves paired phase-bearing relations inside a single quaternion object while remaining exactly recoverable back to grouped complex channels. This establishes a deterministic, information-preserving front end for downstream quaternionic signal processing.

1. Introduction and scope

The paper introduces a deterministic grouped quaternion lift as an internal representation for ordered complex-channel captures without changing the external complex-channel intake contract.

2. Core formal results

  1. Exact recoverability: unpack(lift(Z)) = Z for ordered captures.
  2. Determinism: fixed channel ordering yields a unique grouped lift.
  3. Energy preservation: lane-level additivity extends to capture-wide energy equality.

3. Scope and non-claims

The contribution is representational only: no new sensing physics, no universal hardware advantage, and no claim that lift alone guarantees downstream performance gains.
