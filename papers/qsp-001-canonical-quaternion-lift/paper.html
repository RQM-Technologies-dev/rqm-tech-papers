Quaternion Lifting for Multichannel RF and Sensing Signals

A Canonical Lane Representation for Quaternionic Signal Processing

John G. Van Geem
RQM Technologies
ORCID: 0009-0002-4003-8452

Abstract

This paper defines a canonical quaternion lifting procedure for multichannel RF and sensing signals whose external intake remains standard complex-channel data. Given two complex channels z1[n]=a[n]+ib[n] and z2[n]=c[n]+id[n], the lift forms a quaternion lane q[n]=z1[n]+z2[n]j = a[n]+b[n]i+c[n]j+d[n]k. For larger captures, channels are grouped deterministically into quaternion lanes Q[g,n] in H. The paper formalizes this lift, its inverse unpacking map, odd-channel handling, and representation-level invariants that make it suitable as a canonical internal form. The contribution is representational rather than speculative: instead of flattening multichannel structure too early, the lift preserves paired phase-bearing relations inside a single quaternion object while remaining exactly recoverable back to the grouped complex channels.

1. Introduction

Multichannel RF and sensing systems are often observed through related complex channels rather than isolated scalar streams. This manuscript introduces a canonical quaternion lifting procedure that groups paired complex channels into deterministic quaternion lanes while preserving the external complex-channel intake contract.

2. Scope and external signal model

The external capture is represented as ordered complex samples zc[n] with C channels and N samples per channel. The lift operates only as an internal representation layer after ordering and validation are fixed.

3. Quaternion preliminaries

The paper uses standard quaternion coordinates over H with basis units i, j, k and squared norm |q|2 = qq*. The preliminaries are coordinate-focused and avoid introducing transform conventions at this stage.

4. Canonical quaternion lift

For a two-channel pair, the lane is q[n] = z1[n] + z2[n]j. For general channel count C, channels are grouped deterministically as (2g, 2g+1), with odd-tail handling when C is odd.

5. Recoverability and representation invariants

The manuscript proves exact recoverability via an inverse unpacking map, determinism under fixed ordering, lane-level energy additivity, and capture-wide energy preservation.

6. Why the lift matters for RF and sensing

The representation is motivated by paired or grouped channel structure in dual-polarization, MIMO, phased-array/radar-adjacent captures, and reproducible offline replay workflows.

7. Downstream role in quaternionic signal processing

This paper is representation-first. It positions the lift as a stable front end for later spectral, coherence, and orientation-aware quaternionic processing layers.

8. Scope, limitations, and non-claims

The manuscript explicitly does not claim new sensing physics, universal performance gains, or transform-level completeness in this first paper.

9. Conclusion

The canonical grouped lift is presented as a deterministic, lossless, energy-preserving internal representation for ordered multichannel complex captures.
